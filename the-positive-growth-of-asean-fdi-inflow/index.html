The Positive Growth of ASEAN FDI Inflow

Thomas Shaw

The foreign direct investment (FDI) flows into ASEAN are at an all-time high in 2019 with US$180 billion invested. Even though it fell by 25% to US$135 billion in 2020 given the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, ASEAN remained an attractive investment destination, with its share of global FDI rising from 11.9% in 2019 to 13.7% in 2020. 

Understanding Inward Investment

Multinational corporations usually invest capital in the foreign market to grow their presence or to meet the demand of the local economy. This type of investment is known as inward investment. Foreign direct investment (FDI) is a type of inward investment, in which foreign companies purchases local business or establishes new operations for an existing business. Inward investment help companies grow and improve local economies by bringing employment and infrastructure development.

FDI Inflow For Past 5 Years

The inward foreign direct investment flows into the ASEAN region are on a steady rise for the past 5 years. It reached an all-time high in 2019 when a total of US$180 billion was invested. 

The top FDI recipient in the region is Singapore with a total of US$435.6 billion, followed by Indonesia(US$87.3 billion) and Vietnam(US$74.1 billion). Singapore is one of the easiest cities in the world to do business in because of the tax incentives and exemptions, the pro-business legislation, and the cityâ€™s financial stability. This in turn creates a favorable trade environment for foreign companies.

The financial and insurance sectors are the biggest gainer for the past 5 years as they received more than 30% of total foreign direct investment in the region. Manufacturing and wholesale and retail trade are also one of the highest subsectors receiving FDIs at 25% and 14% respectively.

COVID-19 Impact

The COVID-19 pandemic had halted global trading and the FDI inflow of ASEAN countries is declining. Thailand suffers the most as its FDI inflow was down by 201.2% from US$4.79 billion in 2019 to -US$4.84 billion in 2020. The negative value indicates that the outflow investment exceeds the inflows. Despite the pandemic, some countries in the region manage to increase their FDI inflow such as Brunei, Laos, and Myanmar. Brunei up 54% from US$0.4 billion in 2019 to US$0.6 billion in 2020.
