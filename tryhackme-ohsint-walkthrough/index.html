TryHackMe - OhSINT Walkthrough

Thomas Shaw

OhSINT is a free room on the TryHackMe platform. The objective of this challenge is to use open-source intelligence techniques to obtain the flags.

This is the photo provided for us. Question 1: What is this users avatar of?

We can search our target username (OWoodflint) on Google. We then get three results associated with our target, a Twitter account, a WordPress blog, and a GitHub account. We need to explore it one by one to find the account with an avatar. 

Then, we will find that the avatar of our target Twitter account is a cat.

Ans: cat

Question 2: What city is this person in?

There is a post on the Twitter account that captures our interest. It is a post about a BSSID of an access point near the targetâ€™s house.

We can use WiGLE to see the location. Simply put the BSSID in the search field.

Ans: London

Question 3: Whats the SSID of the WAP he connected to?

This information also can be found by using WiGLE.

Ans: UnileverWifi

Question 4: What is his personal email address?
Question 5: What site did you find his email address on?

We can check out our target GitHub to look for the email address. There is only one repo called `people_finder`. Clicking at the repo, we can get the email address.

Ans: OWoodflint@gmail.com
 Ans: GitHub

Question 6: Where has he gone on holiday?

We need to visit our target blog and the only post there will give us the answer.

Ans: New York

Question 7: What is this persons password?

Looking through Twitter and GitHub, we can't seem to find any information regarding our target passwords. We need to revisit the blog and this time looking at the source code. There is one paragraph with text in white color. As the background of the blog is white, we can't see it normally.

Ans: pennYDr0pper.!
